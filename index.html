Rules

We all know hackathons are awesome and fun but we have to follow these rules to maintain the awesomeness of the event

1. Be respectful and excellent to others
This is, by all means, the most important rule you must abide. We strive for the J-Hacks to be an inclusive and diverse environment. Any misbehavior or action that belittles or degrades other hackers will not be allowed, and the participant will be immediately disqualified and removed from the event. It is just as essential to mention that the final decision belongs to the jury’s discretion.
2. Build something Here and New
All projects presented at the J-Hacks 2015 Hackathon must be developed during the event.
The three categories for the event are: Kinect Hack, Internet of Things (IoT) and Cloud Hack.
You are allowed to use frameworks, tools, APIs and open source libraries made available by the J-Hacks Organizing Committee or that have been available at least for a month.
Remember, this is more than presenting a power point, don’t show just screenshots and slides; present how your project works.
3. Code Audit (is this valid?)
Finalists will have their project's source code audited to make sure it's new, legit and complies with the rules and guidelines.
4. Team size limit
The number of members per team is limited to 4. A participant can only belong to one team.
5. Have fun!
Don't stop repeating this: You are at the Hackathon to have fun! Although there is an element of competition, the J-Hacks Hackathon is a place to experiment, learn new things, meet great people, and build (or break) things from scratch. Don't stress yourself too much, don't get upset if you can't make it, and never quit (even if you don't win). Just have a good time and put your best effort and skills towards your project. Remember, the place where disruption and innovation takes place is in Puerto Rico! ;)
